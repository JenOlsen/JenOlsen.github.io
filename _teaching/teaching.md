My teaching is research-informed and focused on medical devices, engineering design, and accessibility. I aim to make learning engaging and relevant by using real-world examples, while encouraging students to think critically about how engineering can improve lives and create more inclusive technologies. I am a [Fellow of Advance HE (D2)](https://www.advance-he.ac.uk/fellowship/fellowship).

## Newcastle University, UK
- [MEC8056: Medical Devices Regulatory Requirements](https://www.ncl.ac.uk/undergraduate/degrees/module/?code=MEC8056) (Module Lead, since January 2025)
    - As Module Leader for MEC8056, I deliver teaching on the importance of regulatory frameworks in medical devices, drawing on real-world case studies of products that have reached the market yet have given rise to significant patient harm and wider societal concern. The module places a particular emphasis on femtech and women’s health, examining examples such as the Dalkon Shield intrauterine device, the PIP breast implant scandal, and the vaginal mesh scandal.
- [MEC3098: Mechanical Engineering Project](https://www.ncl.ac.uk/undergraduate/degrees/module/?code=MEC3098&year=2026) (Module Contributor, since 2025)
    - As a contributor to MEC3098, I have developed academic skills content designed to enhance accessibility and clarity for students undertaking their first dissertation project. This includes guidance on structuring an engineering dissertation, effectively presenting technical work, and appropriately evidencing and documenting research.
- [PARTNERS: Mechanical Engineering](https://www.ncl.ac.uk/partners/) (Strand Lead, 2025)
